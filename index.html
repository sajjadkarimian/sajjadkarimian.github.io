About me

Sajjad Karimian is a researcher working at the intersection of healthcare data, trustworthy AI, and socio-technical systems. His work focuses on improving data transparency, quality, and reliability in clinical environments where data directly impacts patient safety and decision-making.

With a background in Computer Science and Medical Informatics, he applies data analytics, FAIR principles, and ontology engineering (RDF/OWL) to model and evaluate data-driven systems. His research combines technical methods with Human Factors approaches, working closely with clinical stakeholders to translate real-world challenges into transparent and accountable solutions.

He has contributed to multiple journals and conference publications and received the Best Poster Award at the ACM Human-Centered AI Education & Practice Conference. Alongside research, he has experience in teaching and healthcare IT, bringing a practical systems perspective to his work.
